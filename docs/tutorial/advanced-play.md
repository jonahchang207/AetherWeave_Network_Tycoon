# Advanced Play

## QoS policy (Phase 2+)

Typical queue strategy:

1. **Network control** (ARP keepalives, SPF) — highest priority, tiny bandwidth
2. **Business CIR** — guaranteed minimum
3. **Residential best-effort** — scavenger class during congestion

Misconfigured QoS starves residential customers and wins short-term business revenue — until reviews destroy your brand modifier.

## Traffic engineering during festivals

- Pre-negotiate **temporary transit burst** with upstream guild.
- Enable **caching grimoires** at regional Nexuses (content delivery mechanic).
- Schedule maintenance **after** the event, not before.

## Sub-Aetheric cables (Phase 4)

- Lay **two** paths on diverse repeater chains — kraken events are scripted stress tests.
- Model **latency vs. cost** — northern route may be longer but safer.
- Test **ARP failover** by toggling link down in sandbox mode before going live.

## CLI efficiency bonus

Advanced players may open the **Weaver Terminal**:

```text
nexus-core# configure terminal
nexus-core(config)# router arp 65001
nexus-core(config-router)# neighbor 203.0.113.1 remote-as 64500
```

Correct configs converge faster and unlock **NOC prestige** modifiers. Typos can export invalid routes — use `show ip arp` before commit.
