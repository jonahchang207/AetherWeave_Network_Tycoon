# Economy Tips

## Revenue streams

| Source | Stability | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Residential subscriptions | Steady | Volume play in Verdant Shards |
| Business SLAs | High margin | Penalties hurt — engineer QoS |
| Transit sales (Tier 1) | Massive | Requires global reach |
| Peering settlement | Variable | Usually zero if balanced |

## Costs to watch

- **Transit** — scales with purchased Gbps and utilization penalties
- **Power** — Nexus draw on Mana-Grid; spikes during festivals
- **Maintenance** — crew retainers reduce fiber-cut downtime
- **Depreciation** — sunk CapEx becomes monthly OpEx on dashboard
- **Loan interest** — don’t ignore in Phase 1

## Pricing strategy

- **Undercut rivals** early to grab share — then raise prices after 6 in-game months when switching costs bite.
- Bundle **FTTH** in wealthy districts only; Copper-Weave elsewhere preserves margin.
- Business tier: sell **committed information rate (CIR)** with hard SLA — charge 3–5× residential.

## When to peer vs. buy transit

Peer when:

- You exchange similar traffic volumes with a regional guild
- IXP port cost &lt; projected transit savings over 12 months

Stay on transit when:

- You are **traffic-heavy inbound** (content hoarder) — peers may refuse free peering
- You need reach to regions no peer covers

## Acquisition tips (Phase 3)

Buying a failing ISP:

- Audit their **ARP table** for bogons and leaked prefixes
- Factor **deferred maintenance** (hidden fiber cuts) into offer price
- Keep their brand briefly to avoid mass churn, then migrate to your ARP ASN

## Bankruptcy avoidance checklist

- Maintain **3 months OpEx** cash buffer by mid Phase 2
- Never run without **repair crew** contract in storm regions
- Refinance loan only when MRR &gt; 2× debt service
