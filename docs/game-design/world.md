# World & Lore — Oertha

## The Great Silence

Millennia ago, **Oertha** was united by the **Ancient Loom** — a global magical network for instant communication and shared knowledge. The Loom **shattered** in the **Great Silence**, plunging civilizations into localized dark ages.

Scholars have rediscovered how to manufacture **Ley-Glass**: crystal strands that transmit **Lumina** (light/data) across vast distances. You found a new **Guild of Weavers** to weave the world back together.

## The Aetherial Expanse

Oertha is not a simple globe. It is a **shattered super-continent** separated by **Aether-Seas** — dangerous magical atmospheric oceans. Geography drives cost, risk, and strategy.

### Regions

| Region | Role | Gameplay hook |
|--------|------|----------------|
| **Verdant Shards** | Starting zone — dense, safe floating islands | High population, low wealth; cheap overhead builds; rival startups |
| **Silicon Spires of Zennor** | Commercial capital | Massive bandwidth demand; expensive underground/under-city routing |
| **Abyssal Trench** | Stormy Aether-Sea between hemispheres | Sub-Aetheric cables: extreme cost, specialized ships, repeaters |
| **Frost-Forge Peaks** | Cold mountains | Lower signal attenuation (longer cable reach); avalanches & ice-wyrm fiber cuts |
| **Dune Seas of Kael** | Deserts | Aetheric storms → packet loss unless cables are shielded |

## Fantasy ↔ real-world glossary

| In-world | Real-world analogue |
|----------|---------------------|
| Ley-Glass | Fiber optics |
| Lumina | Data / light / traffic |
| Nexus Node | Router / switch |
| Archon Routing Protocol (ARP) | BGP |
| Incantation Prefix | IP prefix |
| Lumina Exchange Point | IXP |
| Copper-Weave | DSL / coax last-mile |
| Mana-Grid | Power / utility grid |
| Demonic Denial of Service | DDoS |

## Narrative tone

Steampunk guild politics meets network operations center. Success is measured in **uptime**, **peering relationships**, and whether your name is whispered with respect at the Arch-Loom councils — not in slaying dragons (though ice-wyrms may eat your backhaul).
